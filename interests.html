Web Development

According to Brainstation, web development is the tasks associated with creating, building, and maintaining websites and applications that run on a browser. There is a scope of different things under the web development pathway. The three main types of web development are front-end, back-end and full-stack. At the moment I am most interested in front-end development, but I am working on becoming more well rounded and valuable to the industry as a full-stack developer.

I am interested in this career pathway and have been for a while. Prior to this opportunity I was taking a web development bootcamp class on Udemy and have found the basics fun. It has been challenging, but it keeps me mentally stimulated. With further research I also learned that employment for developers will increase about 30.3% between 2021-2031, which allows for more opportunities.

UI/UX Design

UI (User Interface) and UX (User Experience) Designers work together to create a product. UI designers tend more to the aesthetics of of a digital product. They create the look and feel of a website, making it functional, easy to use, and visually appealing- according to flatironschool. UX designers plan the experience a person has when interacting with said product. They focus on the interaction with users have with everyday products and services. They also do a lot of research to make a product easy to use by conducting interviews, testing behaviors, check the flow of the product and test and continue to improve the product over time. Both of these jobs require attention to detail and soft skills.

This field falls hand in hand with web developers, which is why it is why it is something I am interested in. I requires similar skills that can be applied that I know I can master and transfer.

Scrum Master

A scrum mater is a coach and guide for their team and understands the scrum framework, principles, and values. They support continuous team improvement as their duties are facilitating conversations, managing impediments and coaching the team. These soft skills are similar to those of being a teacher. Their responsibilities are to make sure the team understand the use of scrum effectively.

I am interested in this agile path, as it reminds me of my teaching experience. Some of the qualities of this position include: natural focus on the team, enjoying helping people grow, people oriented, facilitating discussions, empowering teams and ensuring that the team understand the priorities.
